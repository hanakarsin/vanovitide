James Gakara: Nakuru Doctor's Casket Placed Between Those of His Kids in Emotional Requiem Mass

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Monday, August 5, 2024
  • James Gakara is said to have killed his two children before attempting to take his life, he later died while receiving treatment
  • During the mass, the deceased's relatives, who were still struggling to come to terms with the painful loss emotionally eulogised them
  • The three will be laid to rest at their family home in Mbaruk, Gilgil sub-county, on Tuesday, September 28

A sombre mood engulfed the Holy Trinity Milimani Catholic Church in Nakuru as the family of James Gakara held a requiem mass for him and his two children.

Gakara, a doctor by profession, is said to have killed his two children before attempting to take his life. He later died while receiving treatment.

During the memorial service held on Monday, September 27, the medic's casket was placed between those of his children laying side by side.

The deceased's relatives, who were still struggling to come to terms with the painful loss of the three, emotionally eulogised them.

In her tribute, Gakara's widow Winnie Odhiambo, the mother of the two late children, reminisced about her good times with them.

Odhiambo said she and Gakara, together with their children, lived together as a happy family.

According to family spokesman Burton Njoroge, the three will be laid to rest at their family home in Mbaruk, Gilgil sub-county, on Tuesday, September 28.

As previously reported, Gakara was found lying unconscious next to his two dead kids in suspected murder and attempted suicide case.

He was rushed to Nakuru Level Five hospital, where he breathed his last on Wednesday, September 22.

The gynaecologist based at Nakuru was later accused of killing his five-year-old Dylan Gakara and Karuana Gakara, three, before attempting to take his own life on the evening of Saturday, September 18, in his Kiamunyi Estate home.

Nakuru county police commander Beatrice Kiraguri confirmed the incident saying several vials of drugs and syringes and a knife were found at the crime scene.

It was reported that after regaining consciousness, the medic admitted to having drugged the minors, leading to their death.

A neighbour claimed that the doctor committed the heinous act after disagreeing with his wife, who allegedly wanted to travel abroad against his will.

Earlier, Odhiambo had emotionally mourned her two children and regretted that she did not protect them enough since she was away.

"My heart is shattered; I failed to protect you my loves. The person you trusted the most betrayed you my darlings, I promise to fight for justice for you; go well my sweethearts," she said.
