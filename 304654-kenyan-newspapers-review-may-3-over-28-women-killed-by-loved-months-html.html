Kenyan newspapers review for May 3: Over 28 women killed by their loved ones in four months

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Monday, April 1, 2024

The country's top five newspapers focused on a number of issues ranging from the damning report on outrageous expenses by Kiambu county government to the rising cases of crimes of passion with women singled out as the biggest casualties.

Another story focused on President Uhuru Kenyatta's shift of focus from China to the US for financial support for the Big Four Agenda. Also, there is a rise in criminal gangs across the country as witnessed in different counties.

The Standard

The Standard newspaper gave a thorough analysis on the ongoing cases of crimes of passion that have been recorded throughout the country with women being the most affected.

Notably, at least 14 women (the most publicised cases) have been killed by their husbands or lovers since January 2019 and this raises a red flag about murderous rage within the family. Experts have blamed the trend on depression, drug abuse, frustration and harsh economic times.

Police records indicate men were the highest offenders in majority of the homicide cases reported across the country. Although their victims are not necessarily female, they have been singled out as their main target for rage.

In 2018, 2,856 homicide cases were reported to the police countrywide, a rise from 2,774 the previous year and 2,751 in 2016, according to official statistics. These figures are most likely to go high in 2019.

Some of the factors that have been identified as the drivers of violence include depression and lack of social and problem solving skills, with the suspects resorting to kill or committing suicide instead of talking out about the issues affecting them.

The Daily Nation

Even before Kenyans forget about China and the KSh 368 billion loan for completion of the Standard Gauge Railway from Naivasha to Kisumu, President Uhuru Kenyatta has now shifted focus to the United States for deals to support the Big Four Agenda.

Next week, the president will send a delegation to the US for negotiation of deals to finance major projects in the country.

According to the report, President Donald Trump's administration has come up with a new strategy to counter the ever growing influence of China in Africa.

In particular, Trump has focused on increasing America investment in the country especially through infrastructure projects that are currently dominated by China and Japan.

Trump is also marketing his Connect Africa initiative which has often been interpreted as counter reaction to China's Belt and Road initiative which has a massive geo-economic and political implications in Africa.

People Daily

The newspaper also focused on the killing of women in the country, with statistics indicating that at least 28 women have been killed by their loved ones for the past four months.

Killing of women or femicide which according to World Health Organisation is mostly committed by partners or ex-partners. In particular, it all starts with sexual abuse, threats and intimidation which finally lead to murder.

Researchers indicate most of the crimes committed are driven by anger issues, fear of abandonment and infidelity suspicions.

The Star

The newspaper has focused on the Auditor General's damning report on outrageous financial expenditure by Kiambu county government.

The report came to the limelight during Governor Ferdinand Waititu's appearance before the Senate Public Accounts and Investments Committee (PAIC).

The county boss would not explain how his administration assigned KSh 973 million for coordination of State House functions, a national government affair.

More questions surfaced when the report revealed another scam in the Education, Gender, Culture and social services ministry which had allocated KSh 804 million to Free Primary Education (FPE) which is again a sole role of the national government.

The county shockingly allocated another KSh 58 million for Kenya-South Sudan peace process.

In his defence, the county boss vehemently denied having made such expenditures and blamed bloggers for cooking false stories to taint his administration.

Taifa Leo

The Swahili newspaper has focused on the rising cases of criminal gangs in the country that are terrorising Kenyans across the country.

Among other gangs include the Gaza, Usiku Sacco and the Mungiki which have been identified as some of the groups that are a source of nightmares to businessmen in different counties across the country.

Notably, these groups consist of young men who are jobless and have resorted to crime to support themselves.

According to the newspaper, an interview with some of the members of the Mungiki revealed the group has now regrouped and is back in the streets especially in Murang'a county.
