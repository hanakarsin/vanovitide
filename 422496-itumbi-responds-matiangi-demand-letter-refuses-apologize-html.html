Itumbi Responds to CS Matiang'i Demand Letter, Refuses to Apologize

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Thursday, April 4, 2024
  • Dennis Itumbi,on Sunday, August 15, responded to the demand letter by Interior CS Fred Matiang'i
  • Itumbi said he won't stop calling the CS a thief because, at his behest was outright theft under his supervision and leadership
  • A few days ago, Matiang'i threatened to sue Itumbi for linking him to the explosive Ruaraka land grabbing case

Blogger Dennis Itumbi has finally responded to interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang’i 's demand letter maintaining that the CS is a thief.

Itumbi's letter

In a response letter seen by TUKO.co.ke Itumbi said Matiangi's a thief and will not stop using the term “Ruaraka land thief” anytime soon.

“… In totality, therefore, the payment of KSh 1,500,000,000 at your behest was outright theft under your supervision and leadership. You sold sand to the ocean for a pound of flesh.

Anybody who conceives, initiates, coordinates and executes theft is a thief by all definitions. Therefore, Minister Fred Okeng’o Matiang’i, you are a thief in the context of “Ruaraka Land Thief” is…. supported by a court decision which you have not appealed against,” the response letter read.

My entire response to your letter dated August 11, 2022, is here attached, Matiang’i he said.

" You have been overheard saying you are sure you will get gagging orders stopping me from calling you a Thief, I remain confident that the Judiciary will hear the substantive matter to a fair conclusion and resist the usual games played by those with no case to present in court and who trust so much in Shortcuts,” he added.

Matiang'i demand letter

On Wednesday, August 11, TUKO.co.ke reported that the CS threatened to sue Itumbi for linking him to the explosive Ruaraka land grabbing case.

The CS wrote a demand letter to Itumbi instructing him to retract a post on his Twitter account that referred to him as “the Ruaraka thief” or face legal action.

The letter written by V.A Nyamondi and Co Advocates directed the blogger to withdraw the tweet and give an unconditional apology for his actions.

“We are instructed to demand, as we hereby do, your immediate and unreserved retraction of all tweets referring to our client as a thief. Our client further demands an unconditional apology for the defamatory statements published via your verified Twitter account in a manner to be approved by our client,” the demand letter reads in part.
