Comedian breaks hearts after announcing end of Churchill Raw show after 10 years

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Monday, January 1, 0001

Comedian Daniel Ndambuki has announced the end of a long time stand up comedy that has seen the rise of many comedians in Kenya, Churchill Raw.

Ndambuki alias Churchill, and the team, has had the program on TV for a couple of years and the season finale aired on Thursday, October 31.

In an Instagram post, Ndambuki shared throwback photos of some of the comedians who were mentored through the show among them the famous DJ Shiti.

"Do you know these faces? Today we celebrate a journey, a journey of nothing but growth and transformation. The midweek dose of fun and laughter comes to and end on NTV today," wrote the comedian.

Jasper Muthomi alias MC Jessy, the long time host to the just concluded show, also took to social media to appreciate fans for their support in his journey throughout the show.

"It’s been an amazing journey hosting Churchill Raw. The Churchill Raw family reunites as we air the season finale. As we restructure the show to be bring you even better content, we just want to thank you for the amazing support that you’ve given us over the years," wrote Jessy on Instagram.

The comedian also acknowledged the impact of the show in nurturing talents and promised fans more.

"Some of the raw talents that have passed through the show have gone to become the movers and shakers of our entertainment industry. Keep it here, don’t move as we plan to serve you more," he concluded.

The season finale was dedicated to talents nurtured from the show among them Steven Oduor (DJ Shiti), Carolyne Wanjiku (Teacher Wanjiku), Sleepy David and Eddie Butita who also graced the show.

Although there is an end to everything that has a start, the news broke many hearts and fans went to social media to express varied sentiments.

Churchill will continue to air every Sunday at NTV despite the end to its sister show.
