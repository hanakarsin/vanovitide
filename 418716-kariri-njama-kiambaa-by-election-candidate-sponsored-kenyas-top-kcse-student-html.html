Kiambaa by-election candidate's hand in the education of Kenyas top KCSE student

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, May 21, 2024
  • The Kariri Njama Foundation sponsored Robinson Wanjala throughout his secondary education at Muranga High School
  • Wanjala enjoyed uninterrupted studies in high school and emerged as the top student nationally
  • Kariri has made a name for helping needy people in the cosmopolitan Kiambaa constituency
  • He is among the top candidates vying for the Kiambaa Constituency seat in this week’s by-election.

When Simiyu Robinson Wanjala was announced as the top student nationally in the 2020 KCSE examinations last May, much was said about those who helped make his dream come true.

But conspicuously missing on the accolades list was Kariri Njama, whose foundation sponsored Wanjala throughout his secondary education at Muranga High School.

Four years ago, Wanjala approached the Kariri Njama Foundation seeking assistance to raise school fees for his secondary education.

He had performed well in his KCPE exams sat at Gachie Primary School in Kiambaa, Kiambu County, but his parents were not in a position to send him to a school of his choice.

Kariri listened to the young boy and requested to meet with the parents.

He assessed the situation and was obliged to help Wanjala on the condition that he will continue doing his very best throughout his high school.

Courtesy of the support, Wanjala enjoyed uninterrupted studies in high school and eventually emerged as the top student in Kenya with an impressive mean score of 87.33.

Yet, it was not by mistake that Kariri did not hog the headlines when his “son” emerged the champion.

He is a down-to-earth man not known for blowing his own trumpet. He always lets his actions to speak louder than his words.

He has invested heavily in the education sector in Kiambaa, with his foundation sponsoring over 300 needy students from cosmopolitan constituencies to various secondary schools across the country.

Kariri is among the top candidates vying for the Kiambaa constituency, whose winner will be decided in the highly anticipated by-election on Thursday, July 15.

He is flying the Jubilee Party flag in the contest that has been narrowed down to a two-horse race, with United Democratic Alliance (UDA) candidate Njuguna Wanjiku being his closest challenger.

When Jubilee Party nominated Kariri as its flag bearer, there was no doubt he is a formidable candidate.

He beat six other hopefuls to the ticket and has demonstrated throughout the campaign trail that he is a man worth his salt.

Nicknamed ‘Chief’, Kariri is a father, entrepreneur, politician, philanthropist, servant leader, and a Christian who has consistently expressed and demonstrated his willingness to serve his Kiambaa people.

He is making his third attempt at the seat that was left vacant following the death of Hon. Paul Koinange, who succumbed to covid-19 earlier this year.

Kariri emerged second when he vied for the position in both 2013 and 2017, losing to Koinange on both occasions.

This means Kariri is a person the constituents know very well; someone they relate with and who understands their needs and aspirations. He is a man at ease with his personality and the people.

His grassroots network under the Kariri Njama Foundation has gained root in 2006 and has seen him launch various projects to empower the youth, women and vulnerable people in Kiambaa.

Wanjala’s KCSE victory is among the success stories that manifest the fruits of Kariri’s work.

In his current quest to secure the Kiambaa seat, Kariri has enjoyed the support of top politicians, among them, sitting and former MPs, Senators, and Governors, keen to get a suitable leader to fill the big shoes left behind by the late Koinange.

His candidature also enjoys the support of President Uhuru Kenyatta, who was a close friend of Koinange.

Kariri was recently invited to State House by Uhuru in a ceremony that was significantly attended by a number of politicians who had declared interest in the seat but dropped out of the race and endorsed Njama.

They included Raymond Kuria, who was vying on the People’s Empowerment Party (PEP) which is led by Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria, and political analyst Charles Munyui who was vying as an independent candidate.

Kariri received another major boost after the Kikuyu Council of Elders (KCE) threw its weight behind him.

Led by their national chairman Wachira Kiago, the influential elders have been doing grassroots campaigns for him.

They have made it clear that only an MP from the ruling party will be able to complete all the projects started by the late Koinange within the one year left before the next general elections.
