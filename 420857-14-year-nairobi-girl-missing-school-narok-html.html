14-Year-Old Nairobi Girl Who Went Missing on Her Way to School Found in Narok

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Friday, May 17, 2024
  • Marion Wangu Mukura did not return home on Friday, July 30, after she had left in the morning to attend school
  • Her teachers said she did report to the school on the day, this leaving her parents worried, beginning her search
  • Two days into going missing, Marion has been found in Narok while safe

It is a relief for the family of 14-year-old Marion Wangu Mukura, who has now been found two days after she was reported missing.

Marion, a pupil at Reby-Peter Memorial Academy at Kahawa West, is said to have never returned home on Friday, July 30, after she left in the morning for school.

Upon their inquiries to her teachers, the teen's parents established she had not reported to the school on the day.

They embarked on her search, appealing to whoever might spot her to report.

Nonetheless, the agony is now over as the family will soon reunite with their daughter, who has been traced to Narok, while safe.

"I'm excited to report that Marion has been found. Yes, in Narok. We thank God for preserving her. Thanks to all friends for the prayers and for circulating the post. Her parents are extremely grateful for all your help. God bless," said Ndugu Nyoro.

Family in agony as 15-year-old daughter goes missing for a year

Meanwhile, another family is still agonising over their 15-year-old daughter, who has been missing since last year.

Martin Shikuku said her lastborn daughter Bella Martin should have been with her family to celebrate her 16th birthday, but that did not happen.

Conversing with TUKO.co.ke's Hillary Lisimba, Shikuku took a walk down memory lane to April 12, 2020, the Easter weekend when his daughter stepped out of home around noon and disappeared into thin air.

"No one sent her anywhere, she just opened the gate and walked out like someone taking a walk," he said.

The family filed a missing person's report vide OB Number 19/13/4/2020, but little has yielded from the frantic search.

At the time of her disappearance, the then 15-year-old Bella had come home for the holiday a few weeks since joining Form One at Kambala Girls’ Secondary School.

Lost his wife earlier

News of Bella's disappearance cut Martin deeper because he has been raising their three children singlehandedly after the death of his wife in 2011.

Martin added that not having the two ladies in his life has left a huge hole in his heart, something that has affected him psychologically.

“All my nights are spent on thinking of where I can look for Bella. I haven't slept for over a year,” the heartbroken father added.

Martin's worry is compounded by the rising cases of missing children, with his glimmer of hope rising whenever the story has the happy ending of a reunion.
