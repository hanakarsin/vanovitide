Best Beef Matoke Recipe in Kenya

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Wednesday, May 8, 2024

This is a popular Kenyan plantain recipe loved by many people. So let’s learn how to cook matoke with meat.

Matoke Ingredients

  • 9 – 10 peeled green bananas chopped into halves
  • Two washed and diced tomatoes
  • A cupful of water
  • One carrot
  • Two cloves of thinly sliced garlic
  • ½ kilo of meat that has been cut in pieces
  • Frying oil
  • One washed and diced hoho/green capsicum
  • Salt
  • A bunch of fresh coriander (dhania)
  • ¼ teaspoon of cayenne pepper

Matoke Cooking Directions

  • Heat the oil in a pan that is large enough to hold all the food.
  • Fry the onions, tomatoes, and garlic.
  • Add spices to give the food the taste you desire.
  • Add the meat and continue frying as you stir until your beef is almost done.
  • Reduce the heat and add the chopped green bananas.
  • Cover the food and simmer over the reduced heat till the meat is done and bananas are tender.
  • Serve your matoke while hot.

    How to cook matoke with potatoes

    You can cook spicy matoke with potatoes in an almost similar fashion to that of beef. You only have to add peeled potatoes and chopped bell pepper on your ingredient list. After cooking your tomatoes and onions until tender, you will then proceed to add garlic and bell pepper and cook for an additional three minutes. After that, you will add your potatoes and a cupful of water and let it cook for approximately five minutes.

    Once the potatoes are cooked, add 1½ cups of water and pour the bananas into the pan then add salt. Continue boiling with reduced heat then simmer when the bananas are nearly cooked. Once done, you can serve the delicacy when hot.

    Health benefits of a mashed matoke recipe

    Now that you know how to cook delicious matoke, we can proceed to the health benefits. Although matoke is simply a yellow banana that is unripened, it has many health benefits sometimes surpassing the ripened variety. Nutritionally, matoke is an excellent source of minerals, fiber, and vitamins. It also contains sugar that helps regulate blood sugar, lower blood cholesterol, and manage weight. Below is a detailed explanation of some of the health benefits of matoke.

    Source of resistance starch

    This is a form of carbohydrate that can’t be broken down by digestive enzymes, therefore, acting more like fiber. Matoke has a high resistant starch content and including it in your diet may lower your chances of getting diabetes as it aids blood sugar control. It also lowers the chances of heart disease since the cholesterol levels in the blood are reduced.

    Source of fiber

    A one-cup serving of matoke has 3.6 grams of fiber, which meets your daily value of 14 percent. The daily value percentage is based on the 2,000-calorie recommendation for a healthy adult. Consuming fiber lowers the risk of heart disease and diabetes. Additionally, it slows digestion, therefore making you feel full for longer, which aids weight control.

    Source of potassium

    Just like eating a ripe yellow banana, matoke has a high potassium content. One cup serving of this delicacy has 534 milligrams of this mineral. By including high-potassium foods in meals, you will aid the control of blood pressure. However, such foods aren’t safe for people with high potassium levels in blood or kidney disease.

    High Vitamin B-6 content

    One cup of matoke contains 39 percent of your daily Vitamin B-6 daily value. The mineral plays a significant role in over 100 enzymatic reactions in the body. Furthermore, it aids the formation of blood hemoglobin and aids in the regulation of blood sugar.
