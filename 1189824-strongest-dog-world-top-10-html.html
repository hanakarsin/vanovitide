Strongest dog in the world - Top 10

Often times large breeds are considered to be the strongest dogs in the world, since large size and muscles provide their power. However, one should not also ignore such breeds as malamutes or huskies, which can often successfully fight with the larger dogs. Top 10 world strongest dogs

Discover the top 10 strongest and most powerful dog in the whole world.

10. The Irish Wolfhound

This is one of the largest dogs, bred specially for wolves hunting. The Irish wolfhound ancestors were used to harass mooses and wild boars. However, in the 17th century they were actively exported from the country, but then the breed was restored. These dogs are large, strong, muscular, move swiftly and easily. When living with the family, they are independent kind giants with a phlegmatic nature. But when being attacked, they can quickly become enraged, have lightning reflexes and hunting instinct. The Irish wolfhound can grow up to 86 cm and can weigh from 45 to 55 kg.

9. The American Bulldog

These dogs are of average size but have a real death grip. They are excellent guardians since they have an inherent distrust of strangers and great fighting instincts. In England, the Bulldogs used to be cattle herders, since their bite was so powerful that it might bring down the bull. They also had no equal rivals at dogfights. American bulldogs can be trained and can become reliable defenders of your family ready even to sacrifice their lives. These dogs can grow from 53 to 68 cm and can weigh from 27 to 55 kilograms.

8. The Akita Inu

This dogs is considered to be the national treasure of Japan, as well as one of the strongest fighting dogs. They have a devoted restrained and calm character, however, they tend to be leaders. The emperors and the royals preferred to have these reliable and loyal friends. The Akita Inu dogs are easy to train, possess natural quick reactions and hunting instincts. Initially, they were bred for hunting reindeer, wild boars and even black bears weighing more than 400 kilograms. These dogs can grow from 58 to 70 cm and can weigh about 35-48 kg.

7. The Caucasian Shepherd Dog

These dogs are fairly willful, though they are very devoted. This is one of the oldest breeds, which is believed to originate from the crossing of a shepherd's dog with a wolf. Thus it inherited freedom, maliciousness and fearless. The Caucasian Shepherd is considered to be a guardian shepherd breed, and it has well-developed hunting instincts. They are mainly used for hunting wolves, as well as bears or wild boars. Since the Caucasian shepherd can defeat the fighters' dog, it is also a reliable bodyguard. Their height can be 65-75 cm, and weight - 40-80 kg.

6. The Alabai

Its official name is the Central Asian Shepherd. This large and friendly dog is also an excellent hunter, watchdog, and a working dog. The Alabai dogs are free and independent, they choose one master and remain loyalty till the end, they will defend everything that is dear to their master even if they would die in such a fight. They have an inherent distrust of strangers, strong grip and impressive size. They can grow to 45-78 cm and weight - 50-80 kg.

5. The Great Dane

A beautiful, strong and noble dog, with a calm temperament and a powerful, slightly frightening appearance. Their ancestors are German mastiffs. Originally it was a working breed, which was taken for hunting large animals. They have a calm and slightly phlegmatic nature, they have natural wit, physical strength and an intolerance to the troublemakers hidden. They are devoted to the owner, loves society, and children. These are tall dogs; their highest breed had a height of 111 cm. Their weight is about 45-90 kg, and their height - 72-80 cm.

4. The Lottatore Brindisino

It was bred in Italy by crossing Cane Corso, Pit Bull Terrier, Rottweiler and Neapolitan Mastiff. It is believed that this dog breed was created for the mafia specifically for dog fights, they possess great strong fighting qualities. Such dogs have a daring temper, ferocity, increased aggression, can easily lose control. These dogs do not fit families with children and people who are looking for dog companion. They are excellent guards and watchdogs. Their height can be up to 50-58 cm, and weight, up to 42-50 kg.

3. The Dogue de Bordeaux

They are strong and large, excellent defenders and guards. In the Middle Ages, these dogs were used to hunt large animals - mooses, wild boars, bears, wolves. They are also very calm and sociable pets within the family. They can grow to about 58-68 cm in height and weigh about 45-55 kg.

2. The St. Bernard

These dogs are impressive with their dimensions, they are very powerful and can carry impressive cargoes for long distances. Previously, these dogs were kept at monasteries high in the mountains, they helped found travelers under the snow and saved them. Now they are working dogs, rescue dogs and guards. St. Bernard dogs are friendly and devoted, with a high intellect. They are a little bit stubborn, with strong defensive instincts. A St. Bernard can weigh up to 55-90 kg and their height is between to 65-90 cm.
