DCI Sleuths Place Police Officer at Murder Scene of Ongata Rongai Conductor Arrested during Curfew H

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, April 23, 2024

- Joshua Mungai was a Matatu conductor at Stage One in Ongata Rongai until his death on April 21 at KNH

- Mungai had been arrested alongside two others identified as Njenga and George over accusation of flouting curfew regulations

- A multi-agency investigation placed police officer Edwin Oscar Okimaru at the murder scene

The homicide team from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) has promised to leave no stone unturned in the murder probe of Ongata Rongai matatu conductor.

Joshua Mungai, who until his death was a Matatu conductor at Stage One in Ongata Rongai, was arrested by police constable Edwin Oscar Okimaru on April 18 at around 10pm.

At the time of arrest, Mungai was in the company of two others only identified as Njenga and George, together with whom he had been accused of violating curfew regulations.

Assaulted, tortured, and dumped

After being assaulted, the three were bundled into a truck of a waiting private car and driven away before being dumped at Tuala area, Kajiado, in the dead of night.

Luckily, George, who was tied up using a rope, managed to free himself and rescued Mungai and Njenga, both of whom had been badly injured, according to the DCI preliminary reports.

George then managed to hail a boda boda rider who was riding past, and together, they helped the deceased who couldn’t walk on the motorbike.

Using a bodaboda hailing app, George called for a rider hoping to assist Mungai to the hospital.

However, fearing they could again bump into the officers, George and Njenga left their friend near Nazarene University and went to separate ways.

The following morning on April 19, a passer-by discovered frail Mungia and offered to take him to Ongata Rongai Health Center; details at the facility showed he received first aid.

The caregivers advised Mungai to be transferred to Kenyatta National Hospital as he was in critical condition.

He passed on two days later.

Forensic analysis

The family of the deceased appealed to the DCI office to take over the matter from Ongata Rongai police station, where Mungai's friends had filed the case.

A multi-agency investigation that later placed the police officer at the murder scene corroborated witnesses' accounts that the deceased was killed.

Mungai suffered chest injuries and broken ribs and bones, indicating that he had been tortured before his death.

"The Directorate of Criminal Investigations reiterates that we shall leave no stone unturned, in the investigation of this heinous murder, irrespective of whether the perpetrators are police officers or not," DCI assured.

It was not immediately established if the Dabel police station-based officer was alone; however, detectives are still pursuing other crucial leads.

The arrest came barely a week after Embakasi DCI boss Simon Mwongela was picked up for questioning for allegedly shooting dead a watchman in Kayole, Nairobi county.

Mwongela was arrested on Wednesday, May 13, according to a statement issued by Inspector General of Police Hillary Mutyambai.
