Priests unearth 2 magical pots at Mwingi's notorious blackspot where 10 students perished

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, June 22, 2024

- The cleansing ritual was performed by leaders from different churches in Kitui county

- The pots were discovered after a series of prayers and fasting

- The pots containing skulls of human heads are believed to be responsible for accidents at the spot

- The ritual was performed weeks after 10 pupils lost their lives in a tragic accident at the said spot

Church leaders from Kitui County on Monday, August 27, unearthed two pots at Mwingi's notorious blackspot believed to be causing frequent accidents at the scene.

The pots are believed to be containing skulls of people who died long time ago and are behind the accidents.

According to an eye witness, the leaders from different churches did a fasting prayer to determine the root cause of fatal accidents that frequently occur in Mwingi.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke on phone, one of the witnesses said the church leaders had been guided by God after fasting and praying.

"The leaders have in the recent past held several fasting-prayers of fasting and we believe God finally answered their prayers as He showed them where the two pots were," she said.

"From today hence forth there will be no serious accidents as we have been witnessing in the recent past," she added.

The ritual-like ceremony happened at Kanginga area along Mwingi-Garissa road.

The ritual happened barely weeks after at least 10 pupils of St Gabriel Primary School in Mwingi, perished in a tragic road accident at the said blackspot while returning from a trip in Mombasa.

The bizarre-like cleansing is the third to be reported after similar activity was done at Salgaa along Nakuru-Nairobi highway and another one at Kamukuywa, a notorious blackspot along Kitale-Bungoma highway.

All eyes will now be looking at Machakos to prove weather or not the prayers will work after which-doctors attempted to curb accident at Kamukuywa and Salga failed.
