Tanzanian Administrator Rejects Expensive Car, Uses Part of Funds to Buy 12 Motorbikes for Her Junio

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, July 19, 2024
  • Kigoma's chief administrator Fatna Lay took the remaining money after she had bought her car to procure motorbikes for her juniors at work
  • She said the motorbikes will be useful to the division administrators who will be using them while traversing their areas of jurisdiction
  • Her gesture was received with varied reactions, with a section of Tanzanians lauding her for what they said was patriotism

A Tanzanian administrator has surprised many by her kind gesture after going for a modest car so that she could use the remainder of the money allocated to her to procure motorbikes for her juniors.

Fatna Lay, the city manager of Kasulu Kigoma, refused to use all the money allocated for her work car purchase, and instead, she divided the money and bought 12 motorbikes for her district executives.

According to Millard Ayo, Lay was given TSh 210 million (KSh 9 million), to buy a new car due to her previous one being worn out after three years of usage.

However, the officer only used 162 million, equivalent to KSh 7 million, to buy herself a car, using the remaining TSh 47 million (KSh 2 million) to buy the 12 motorbikes for her juniors.

The officer handed over the motorbike to her juniors on Friday, July 16.

Many social media users praised the administrator for being kind and selfless, being mindful of her juniors.

Those lauding her called on the others holding the positions of power to emulate Fatna's kind and selfless being.

Nevertheless, a section said Fatna's was a stunt given she had used a huge chunk of the initial money to purchase her own car.

They argued the monies were taxpayers' and thus, there was no big gesture as it had been made to appear.
