Bungoma Governor Wangamati Under Fire for Calling, Threatening Journalist over Damning Report

Bungoma governor Wycliffe Wangamati has found himself in the soup after allegedly calling and threatening a journalist over a damning report.

It has emerged that the governor threatened the Royal Media Services (RMS) reporter Jimmy Simiyu over a story he has been pursuing.

In a statement by the Media Council of Kenya, the council strongly condemned the actions of Wangamati.

The council stated that they were aware of the allegations that Wangamati had called the reporter to threaten him after an editor from the media tipped him off about the story.

"The council is aware of the allegations that the governor called Simiyu to threaten him after an editor from the media house tipped him off about the story that the reporter had filled over an event held in Bungoma county," the statement read.

According to the board, they advised the victim to report the matter to the police station, which he did and was recorded as OB 16/13/6/21 at the Bungoma Police Station.

The council also told that the governor to desist from threatening reporters and respect the independence and the freedom of all journalists.

They also urge the Inspector General of Police to intervene by ensuring that the matter is expeditiously investigated with the view of bringing all those involved in all the alleged violation to account.

At the same time, they directed Royal Media Services to take measures and establish the truth behind the allegations that one of its editors was involved in the transgression.
