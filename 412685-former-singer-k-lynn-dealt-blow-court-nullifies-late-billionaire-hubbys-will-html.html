Former Singer K-Lynn Dealt Blow as Court Nullifies Late Billionaire Hubby's Will

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, July 25, 2024

- The court rejected a will that late prominent Tanzanian businessman Reginald Mengi allegedly wrote

- The ruling was a blow to his second wife, Jacqueline Ntuyabaliwe Mengi, who was known as K-Lynn during her singing days

- Mengi's alleged will had given his estate to K-Lynn and their twin children and disinherited Mengi's other children

Former singer K-Lynn, real name Jacqueline Ntuyabaliwe Mengi, has suffered a blow after High Court in Tanzania nullified a will that was allegedly written by her late billionaire husband, Reginald Mengi.

Judge Yose Mlyambina termed Reginald Mengi's alleged will null and void, saying it did not meet the legal requirements to be considered valid.

One of the late billionaire’s children, Abdiel Reginald Mengi, filed a petition challenging the will’s legitimacy. Abdiel was joined in the suit by his uncle Benson Benjamin Mengi.

In their court filings, they challenged the will and presented six grounds for their objection.

They argued that the will was not sealed and the signature was different from the one Mengi usually used. The family further said Mengi’s relatives and first wife did not witness the signing.

They also claimed that Mengi could not draw the purported will as he faced severe health issues since 2016.

The ruling concluded a case filed in 2019 by four people seeking to be appointed trustees of Mengi’s estate as per the nullified will.

Blow to K-Lynn

The ruling was a blow to K-Lynn, who unsuccessfully requested to be joined in the case. The court rejected her application thrice.

The petitioners claimed the will bequeathed Mengi’s estate to K- Lynn and their twin children. They argued that the will also disinherited Mengi’s other children.

Love story

Mengi, who died on May 2, 2019, in Dubai, narrated to Citizen TV how he met Jacqueline, who is also a former Miss Tanzania.

According to the media mogul, he made the first move in the UK, where Jacqueline attended a music show.

Mengi, whose conglomerate was estimated to be worth at least KSh 59 billion by Forbes, said she rejected his advances.

He jetted back to Tanzania months later, and she accepted his proposal. They tied the knot in a lavish ceremony held in Mauritius.

Mengi reportedly owned 11 newspapers and television stations through his IPP Media Group.
