Israeli ambassador calls for direct flight to the holy land as Kenyan pilgrims flock Jerusalem for E

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Sunday, April 28, 2024

As Christians throng Jerusalem city for Easter celebrations, Israeli Ambassador Noah Gal Gendler has called on the national carrier to initiate direct flights for Kenyans to save on costs and time.

Jerusalem is known for its tradition and history, being one of the oldest cities in the world and considered holy to the three major Abrahamic religions of Judaism, Christianity and Islam.

Over four million people from across the world visit the city annually during the Easter holidays and make stops at holy sites to mark commemoration of the death, crucifixion and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

"There is a big market, many people want to go to Israel and our people want to come to Kenya. The big problem is the lack of direct flights, direct flights will save cost and time," said Gendler who spoke to K24 Tv.

On Friday, April 19, faithfuls who visited the city marched in procession making stops at the 14 stations of the way of the cross before finally holding a church service at the Holy Sepulchre.

Also, there is a section of the Wailing Wall where faithfuls gather and say their problems while touching the wall and making their prayers. Most importantly, no visitor leaves the city without drinking or even carrying the holy water.
