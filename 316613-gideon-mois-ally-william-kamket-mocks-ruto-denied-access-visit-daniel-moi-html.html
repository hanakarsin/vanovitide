Gideon Moi's ally William Kamket mocks Ruto for being denied access to visit Daniel Moi

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Saturday, May 18, 2024

- Kamket was responding to his critics who have been calling him watchman at Mzee Moi's home

- The Tiaty MP said he was proud to serve as a guard at Moi's Kabarak home because he will have a chance to lock out Ruto

- The vocal lawmaker said he will allow all other national leaders to visit the retired president apart from the DP.

Tiaty MP William Kamket has taken a swipe at Deputy President William Ruto after his attempt to visit retired president Daniel Moi at his Kabarak home hit a sag severally.

In an apparent response to his critics who had branded him a watchman, Kamket said he has officially taken up the role of a guard at Mzee Moi's Kabarak gate and vowed to ensure the DP does not access the homestead at any given time.

The lawmaker who was speaking during a peace meeting held at Chemolingot in Tiaty, Baringo county on Friday, September 27, said as a security guard at the home of the retired president, he will allow all other leaders enter the compound about from Ruto.

" I have no problem being called a watchman of a homestead that is blessed. So as long as I will be manning the gate at Kabarak and President Uhuru Kenyatta comes I will open for him, when Raila visits I will open, when Kalonzo knocks I will usher him in and so is Mudavadi, Atwoli and Joho. I believe you know the person I will not allow him in, yes that one" said Kamket in apparent reference to Ruto

The KANU lawmaker is one of the DP 's fiercest critics in Rift Valley and has openly been accusing him of corruption and poor leadership that has tainted the image of the community.

The first time MP led an onslaught on Ruto over accusations that he conspired with cartels from the National Cereals and Produce Board (NCPB) and defrauded maize farmers from Rift Valley their hard-earned income.

Kamket has also opposed Ruto's 2022 presidential bit and has thrown his weight behind Gideon Moi whom he has often claimed is suitable and more popular than the DP.

The political supremacy between Gideon and Ruto has been simmering and creating more rift in the expansive and voter-rich region which has turned out to be a crucial hub for whoever wants to assent to power in the country.

The two Rift Valley heavyweights hardly see each other eye to eye and their hostility for each other played out during the funeral of Gideon's elder brother, Jonathan Toroitich.

It was reported the Baringo senator abandoned served lunch upon realising Ruto was on the way and would meet him at the table.

The enmity has also seen the deputy president's attempt to visit Moi thwarted several times in what many observers have claimed is an orchestration of Gideon.
